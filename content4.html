NUTRITION
Welcome to our guide on Nutrition and Diet! Here, we'll explore how the food you eat impacts your health and well-being. A balanced diet is like fuel for your body, providing the essential nutrients needed for optimal function and overall health. Join us on this journey to discover the transformative power of good nutrition.

What is a Balanced Diet?

A balanced diet includes a variety of foods from all food groups, including fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Portion control is important to ensure you're not consuming too much or too little of any nutrient.

Types of nutrition

Carbohydrates

Provide energy for the body and are found in foods like grains, fruits, and dairy products.

Proteins

Essential for building and repairing tissues and are found in foods like meat, fish and eggs.

Fats

Provide energy, supports cell growth, help absorb vitamins and are found in oils, and avocados.

Vitamins

Essential for various bodily functions , found in fruits, vegetables, and dairy products.

Water

Essential for staying hydrated , It also helps with digestion and absorbing nutrients.

Nutritional Needs

Nutritional needs vary depending on factors such as age, gender, activity level, and overall health. For example, pregnant women require additional nutrients to support the growth and development of the baby, while athletes need more energy to fuel their performance. Older adults may have different nutrient needs due to changes in metabolism and nutrient absorption.

Common Myths about Nutrition and diet

  • Fat makes you fat: Fat is high in calories, but eating the right amounts of healthy fats can be part of a balanced diet.
  • Carbs are the enemy: Carbohydrates are a really important source of energy for the body.
  • Need to detox or cleanse regularly: Extreme detox or cleanse diets can be harmful .
  • All fats are bad: it's important to limit saturated fats, but healthy fats are essential for good health.
  • Skipping meals helps with weight loss: Skipping meals can lead to overeating later in the day and can slow down your metabolism.
